LIMBY Hawaiʻi

Locals in My Backyard Hawaiʻi is dedicated to ensuring kanaka and kamaʻāina have a future in Hawaiʻi nei by developing, supporting, and advocating for policy that will keep them here.

Locals are less and less able to own a home in their own home.

Local incomes aren't keeping up with (or driving) home prices.

In the 1970s we grew our housing supply by more than 100% and yet prices soared more than the CONUS.
In the 1990s home production was minimal but home prices fell.
Why? It was the only time outside demand stalled.
